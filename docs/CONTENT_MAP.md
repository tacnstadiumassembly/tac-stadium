# CONTENT MAP — WHAT GOES ON EACH PAGE

> **What is this document?**
> A simple guide that explains what each page is for and what information it should show.
> Written in plain English. No technical words. No code.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

For each page, you will find:

1. **Purpose** — Why this page exists
2. **What the visitor sees** — The information shown on the page
3. **What the visitor should do** — The action we want them to take

Think like a first-time visitor who has never heard of this church before.

---

## PAGE 1: HOME

### Purpose

The home page is the **front door** of the website.

It answers the question: _"What is this place, and should I visit?"_

This is usually the first page someone sees. It must make a good impression quickly.

### What the Visitor Sees

- **Welcome message** — A warm greeting that says who we are
- **Church name** — The Apostolic Church Nigeria – Stadium Assembly
- **Location** — Ogbomosho, Oyo State, Nigeria
- **Service times** — When Sunday services happen
- **A short description** — What to expect (friendly, faith-based, family-welcome)
- **A big button** — "Plan Your Visit" (the main action)
- **Quick links** — Watch online, upcoming events, contact us
- **Footer** — Address, phone number, social media links

### What the Visitor Should Do

**Click "Plan Your Visit"** to learn more about attending in person.

If they're not ready to visit, they might:

- Click "Watch Online" to see a service first
- Scroll down to learn more about the church

---

## PAGE 2: ABOUT

### Purpose

The about page builds **trust**.

It answers the question: _"Who are these people, and what do they believe?"_

A visitor may not feel comfortable visiting a church they know nothing about. This page helps them feel confident.

### What the Visitor Sees

- **Who we are** — A short paragraph about the church and its community
- **Our mission** — What the church is trying to do (in simple words)
- **Our vision** — What the church hopes to become
- **What we believe** — Core beliefs (Bible, Jesus, prayer, community)
- **Our leadership** — Who leads the church (Pastor's name, maybe a photo)
- **Our history** — A brief background (optional, can be short)
- **A button** — "Plan Your Visit" (reinforcing the main goal)

### What the Visitor Should Do

**Feel confident** that this is a trustworthy, welcoming church.

Then click "Plan Your Visit" to take the next step.

---

## PAGE 3: SERMONS

### Purpose

The sermons page lets people **experience the church from home**.

It answers the question: _"What kind of teaching happens here?"_

Someone who is unsure about visiting may want to watch a sermon first. This page gives them that option.

### What the Visitor Sees

- **Page title** — "Sermons" or "Messages"
- **Short introduction** — A sentence inviting them to listen and grow
- **List of sermons** — Each sermon shows:
  - Title (e.g., "Walking in Faith")
  - Preacher name (e.g., "Pastor Bode")
  - Date (e.g., "January 19, 2026")
  - Scripture reference (e.g., "Hebrews 11:1")
  - A link or button to watch the video (YouTube)
- **A note** — "Join us in person for the full experience"
- **A button** — "Plan Your Visit"

### What the Visitor Should Do

**Watch a sermon** to get a feel for the church's teaching.

If they like what they see, click "Plan Your Visit."

---

## PAGE 4: EVENTS

### Purpose

The events page keeps people **informed** about what's happening.

It answers the question: _"Is there anything special coming up?"_

This is useful for both first-time visitors (who might attend a special event) and regular members (who want to stay updated).

### What the Visitor Sees

- **Page title** — "Upcoming Events"
- **Short introduction** — "Stay connected with what's happening at Stadium Assembly"
- **List of events** — Each event shows:
  - Event name (e.g., "Annual Thanksgiving Service")
  - Date and time (e.g., "Sunday, February 15, 2026 at 9:00 AM")
  - Location (e.g., "Church Auditorium")
  - Short description (one or two sentences)
  - A link for more details (optional)
- **A message if no events** — "No upcoming events. Check back soon!"
- **A button** — "Plan Your Visit" (always offer the main action)

### What the Visitor Should Do

**See what's coming up** and consider attending.

If an event interests them, they might plan to attend that specific event.

---

## PAGE 5: PLAN A VISIT

### Purpose

This is the **most important page** on the website.

It answers the question: _"What do I need to know before I show up?"_

A first-time visitor has many worries:

- What should I wear?
- Where do I park?
- Will I feel awkward?
- What about my kids?

This page removes those worries and makes them feel welcome before they even arrive.

### What the Visitor Sees

- **Warm welcome message** — "We can't wait to meet you!"
- **Service times** — When to come
  - Sunday School: 8:00 AM
  - Main Service: Immediately after (around 9:00–9:30 AM)
  - Service ends around 12:00 PM
- **What to expect** — What happens during a service
  - Worship and singing
  - Bible-based teaching
  - Friendly atmosphere
- **What to wear** — "Come as you are. No dress code."
- **Parking information** — "Street parking available nearby. Arrive 10–15 minutes early."
- **Children and youth** — "We have programs for kids and teens."
- **Location** — Church address with a map
- **A form** — "Let us know you're coming" (optional)
  - Name
  - Phone number
  - Email (optional)
  - Planned visit date
  - Message (optional)
- **A confirmation** — "We'll be ready to welcome you!"

### What the Visitor Should Do

**Feel prepared and excited** to visit.

Optionally, fill out the form so the church can greet them personally.

---

## PAGE 6: CONTACT

### Purpose

The contact page makes it **easy to reach the church**.

It answers the question: _"How do I get in touch?"_

Someone might have a question, need directions, or want to speak with someone before visiting.

### What the Visitor Sees

- **Page title** — "Contact Us"
- **Warm message** — "We'd love to hear from you."
- **Church address** — Full address with city and state
- **Phone number** — A number someone will answer
- **Email address** — For written messages
- **WhatsApp** — For quick chat (common in Nigeria)
- **Service times** — A reminder of when services happen
- **A map** — Embedded Google Map showing the location
- **A contact form** — For sending a message
  - Name
  - Email
  - Phone (optional)
  - Message
- **Social media links** — Facebook, Instagram, YouTube

### What the Visitor Should Do

**Reach out** with questions or just to say hello.

Or simply get directions and visit in person.

## THE VISITOR'S JOURNEY

Here is how a typical first-time visitor might move through the website:

```
1. Finds the church on Google
         ↓
2. Lands on HOME page
         ↓
3. Clicks "About" to learn more
         ↓
4. Watches a SERMON to see what it's like
         ↓
5. Clicks "Plan Your Visit"
         ↓
6. Reads what to expect, feels comfortable
         ↓
7. Visits the church on Sunday ✓
```

Every page should gently guide them toward **visiting in person**.
